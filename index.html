World

After the Dark Age, one last New God remained

For nearly two thousand years, Terra has lived without money. In most communes, resources flow freely by design—needs are guaranteed, and “work” is contribution instead of survival.

Children aren’t raised by nuclear families. Almost everyone is created and raised through democratic Parenting Guilds. These are tribes of collective caretaking, bioengineering, and living covenants that carry forward the blessings of the Old Gods.

  • Communes plan production together instead of competing in markets.
  • Debt, rent, and wages are outlawed relics of the Dark Age.
  • Spiritual traditions persist as plural, syncretic cults rather than empires.

Prophets, seers, and revelators warned that the New Gods would return to remake humanity through domination. To prepare, Terra built Phoenix Academy, a democratic university city-state with an acceptance rate near 0.1%, where engineered demigods train for a war of divinity: how to murder a deity before it rewrites history again.

The first arc follows Aura Nivoria as she arrives at Phoenix and is pulled into sparring culture, Parenting Guild honor, and the academy’s real purpose: preparing students for the coming conflict with the New Gods. Alongside new allies and rivals, Aura begins to uncover the deeper systems behind Phoenix—including Phia, a war-built AGI tied to the academy’s planning and survival.
